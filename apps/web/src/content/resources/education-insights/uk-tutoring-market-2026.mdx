### Competitive Dynamics

**Marketplace vs. Agency:**

Marketplaces **winning** because:
- Lower commission (10-15% vs. 30-50%)
- More tutor choice
- Transparent reviews
- Direct tutor relationships

Agencies **surviving** by:
- Managed service (agency handles matching, scheduling)
- Corporate/bulk contracts
- Safeguarding emphasis
- Consistent quality control

**Marketplace vs. Independent:**

Marketplace **advantages:**
- Lead generation
- Trust & credibility
- Payment security
- Review social proof

Independent **advantages:**
- Zero commission
- Direct client relationships
- Pricing flexibility
- Can poach marketplace clients

**Trend:** Tutors use marketplaces to build initial client base, then convert some to private clients. Marketplaces respond with better retention tools and exclusive tutor benefits.

## Challenges Facing the Industry

### Regulation & Quality Control

**Current regulatory landscape:**
- **No formal regulation** of tutoring industry
- **Self-regulation** by professional bodies (The Tutors' Association)
- **DBS checks** not legally required (though industry standard for child tutoring)
- **No qualification requirements** to tutor

**Risks:**
- Unqualified tutors
- Safeguarding concerns
- Inconsistent quality
- Misleading claims

**Potential future regulation:**
- Mandatory DBS for under-18 tutoring
- Qualification requirements for specific subjects/levels
- Advertising standards enforcement
- Title protection ("qualified tutor" designation)

### Market Saturation in Key Regions

**London tutor oversupply:**
- More tutors than demand in some subjects/levels
- Price compression
- Difficult for new tutors to establish

**Rural undersupply:**
- Limited local tutor options
- High prices
- Online tutoring solving this gap

### Economic Sensitivity

**Recession risk:**
- Tutoring is discretionary spending
- Potential 15-25% demand reduction in severe downturn
- BUT: Historical resilience (small dip in 2008-09 crisis, fully recovered by 2011)

**Parental priorities:**
- Education spending often protected even in tough times
- May switch to cheaper options (online, less frequent) rather than stop entirely
